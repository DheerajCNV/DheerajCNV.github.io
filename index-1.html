About Me

Hello! I am Dheeraj, a passionate robotics engineer completing my Master's at Arizona State University. My interests lie at the intersection of Autonomous Systems, Aerial Robotics, and Machine Learning, with a strong focus on Perception, Motion Planning, and Intelligent Control. I received my Bachelor's in Mechanical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Tirupati in May 2023.

I love working hands-on with hardware and software and enjoy building systems that bridge theory and real-world applications. My current research at ASU involves UAV-based hyperspectral imaging for land cover and land use change (LCLUC) analysis, where I integrate deep learning models for environmental monitoring.

Over the years, I've worked on a variety of interdisciplinary projects — from developing drone swarms and remote sensing tools to simulating robotic manipulators and production systems. Whether it's programming autonomous flight paths, modelling robotic dynamics in MATLAB, or creating 3D terrain maps using ROS, I thrive at the crossroads of robotics, software, and innovation.

Recent Work

Simulink-Based Autonomous UAV for Target Tracking and Landing

Developed a Simulink model for the Parrot Mambo Minidrone to autonomously track and land on a moving target.

i. Tuned control parameters on the Parrot Mambo Minidrone to achieve stable flight with response times under 200 ms

ii. Applied adaptive color thresholding for reliable target detection under lighting variations of up to ±30% intensity

iii. Designed a sub-matrix vision algorithm enabling real-time tracking at 15 FPS and precision landings within ±10 cm of a moving target

Autonomous Mobile Manipulation using SLAM-Enabled TurtleBot

Implemented SLAM for autonomous navigation on a TurtleBot with LiDAR and a 4-DOF robotic arm for pick-and-place tasks.

i. Mapped ~40 m² environments with ±5 cm localization accuracy using 2D LiDAR SLAM

ii. Achieved >85% success over 20+ autonomous navigation trials with dynamic obstacle handling

iii. Executed pick-and-place tasks within a 1 m reach, averaging less that 1 min per cycle

Development of a Sensor-Guided 4-Wheel Omni-Drive Robot (Arduino Controlled)

Built an Arduino-controlled 4-wheeled omni-directional robot for holonomic motion and precise navigation.

i. Implemented inverse kinematics for 4-wheel configuration, enabling full 360° translation with ±5° orientation accuracy

ii. Programmed PID-based speed control to maintain target velocities up to 0.6 m/s with ±3% regulation error

iii. Integrated ultrasonic and IR sensors for obstacle detection and task execution with sensor response times under 100 ms

Electric Mobility System
Prototyping

Designed and integrated an electric drive system using embedded controls, applying core principles of robotic mobility.

i. Built a closed-loop throttle control using sensor feedback and microcontroller logic for precise actuator response

ii. Integrated a battery pack with BMS, gaining hands-on experience in safe power delivery for mobile systems

iii. Modeled and assembled drivetrain components from CAD to hardware, bridging design and physical prototyping
